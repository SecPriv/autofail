This calendar displays all known number station broadcasts in an accessible fashion. All events are displayed in the UTC time zone; the current UTC time may be found above the viewer.

The color scheme for stations is as follows:

  • E06: Analog voice
  • M12: Morse code
  • F01: Digital modes

Clicking on a station name leads to the Priyom page about the said station, where you may learn about its transmission format, habits, and recent activity.

[Target: ...] is used to note broadcasts that are unlikely to be heard in Western Europe. Instead, they might be heard on one of many receivers in the area mentioned.

Search is used to note broadcasts whose frequencies are not currently known. It is common for stations to move only a few hundred kilohertz to a few megahertz away from the last used frequency.

Priyom IRC channel / Discord server

Meet the Priyom team in our IRC chatroom! If you have your own client, connect to the Libera network and join the #priyom channel. If you are on Discord, you can find us there as well. Both chat rooms are bridged together and have the same content, you're not missing out anything by being in one rather than the other.

However, you are missing out if you're not joining the chat at all. Priyom without IRC is like shortwave without sound :-)

Wait-what is this?

Priyom is an international organization intending to research and bring to light the mysterious reality of intelligence, military and diplomatic communication via shortwave radio: number stations.

Join the show!

Can I join the Priyom team?

We love new members! There are more stations than we are able to cover, and we always need more ears, covering the gaps. If you're interested, have a listen online, join in on our IRC channel, and get to know the team. Don't be afraid to ask questions - we're more than happy to explain. If you decide to get into shortwave as a hobby, and would like to contribute to Priyom, join in and send us recordings and transcriptions, and we will be welcoming you as a new member.
