In April 2026, IEEE hosted two events celebrating innovation and excellence in engineering and tech. The IEEE Laureate Forum brought together emerging innovators, award recipients, and global leaders for conversations on the future of technology and talent. The IEEE Honors Ceremony featured IEEE’s highest-level awards, including the prestigious IEEE Medal of Honor.

IEEE Impact Challenge

Learn How IEEE Is Empowering Emergency & Relief Workers

IEEE is for those who imagine boldly, engineer responsibly, and advance technology for humanity. The IEEE Impact Challenge, a series of challenges, is a global call to action to help inspire tomorrow’s engineers and technologists to design a better world for all, and to turn expertise into innovation. IEEE is currently looking for visionary teams led by IEEE members to help enable emergency and relief workers to make more informed decisions during emergencies.

Conferences

2026 IEEE Power & Energy Society General Meeting

The 2026 IEEE Power & Energy Society General Meeting (PESGM) is the premier power engineering conference. It will bring together practicing power engineers and academics from all over the world. The aim of the conference is to provide an international forum for experts to promote, share, and discuss various issues and developments in the field of electrical power engineering.

Region 8 Virtual Career Fair

The IEEE Virtual Career Fair will take place on 3 June 2026. It connects students, young professionals, and experienced engineers with leading companies and recruiters across a vibrant and fast-growing technology ecosystem. Whether you are just starting your career, exploring new opportunities, or looking to connect with top engineering and technology talent, the IEEE Virtual Career Fair is designed to support workforce development and meaningful industry engagement.

Simulations

IEEE Senior member Sena Kizildemir uses simulations to study how buildings fail under extreme events, such as impacts and explosions. “Simulations help us understand what could happen before it occurs in real life,” she says, “to be able to better plan for it.” Back when the two airplanes hit the World Trade Center on 11 September 2001, skyscrapers had not been designed or constructed with that kind of catastrophic structural failure in mind.  “You’re creating something to help people. My favorite question to answer is, ‘Can you make this better or easier?’
